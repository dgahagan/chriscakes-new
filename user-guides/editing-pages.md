# Editing Pages

Pages are the main content areas of your website like "About Us," "Services," "Catering," etc. This guide shows you how to update these pages with new text, images, and formatting.

## Viewing Your Pages

1. Open the Studio: **www.chriscakesofmi.com/studio**
2. Click **"Pages"** in the left menu
3. You'll see all your website pages

Common pages you might have:
- About Us
- Services
- Catering
- FAQ
- Contact

## Editing a Page

### Step 1: Open the Page
1. Click **"Pages"** in the left menu
2. Click on the page you want to edit
3. You'll see the page editor

### Step 2: Understanding the Editor

Pages use a "rich text" editor that works similarly to Microsoft Word or Google Docs. You can:

- Add headings
- Write paragraphs
- Make text **bold** or *italic*
- Create bulleted or numbered lists
- Add images
- Create links

### Basic Text Editing

**To add or edit text:**
1. Click in the "Content" area
2. Type or edit your text just like in a word processor
3. Press Enter to create new paragraphs

**To format text:**
- **Bold**: Select text, click the **B** button (or Ctrl+B / Cmd+B)
- *Italic*: Select text, click the *I* button (or Ctrl+I / Cmd+I)
- Headings: Use the dropdown to select "Heading 2", "Heading 3", etc.

### Adding Headings

Headings help organize your content into sections:

1. Put your cursor where you want a heading
2. Use the style dropdown to select a heading level:
   - **Heading 2**: Main section headings
   - **Heading 3**: Subsection headings
   - **Normal**: Regular paragraph text

**Example structure:**
```
Welcome to ChrisCakes (Heading 2)
We've been serving pancakes by the millions since 1969... (Normal text)

Our Specialties (Heading 2)
We specialize in breakfast and event catering for groups of all sizes... (Normal text)

Breakfast Catering (Heading 3)
All-you-can-eat pancake breakfasts perfect for any event... (Normal text)

Lunch & Dinner Catering (Heading 3)
From hot dogs to BBQ, we've got your event covered... (Normal text)
```

### Creating Lists

**Bulleted List:**
1. Click where you want the list
2. Click the bullet list button
3. Type your items, pressing Enter after each one
4. Press Enter twice to exit the list

**Example:**
- All-you-can-eat pancake breakfasts
- Corporate event catering
- School and fundraising events
- Lunch and dinner catering packages

**Numbered List:**
1. Click the numbered list button instead
2. Same process as bulleted lists

### Adding Images

1. Click where you want to add an image
2. Look for an "Add Image" or **+** button
3. Upload your image from your computer
4. Add "alt text" (a description of the image for accessibility)
5. The image will appear in your content

**Image Tips:**
- Use high-quality photos (at least 1000 pixels wide)
- Images of your actual events and food service are best
- Keep file sizes reasonable (under 2-3 MB if possible)
- Add descriptive alt text like "ChrisCakes pancake breakfast setup at corporate event"

### Adding Links

To link to another website or page:

1. Select the text you want to turn into a link
2. Click the link button (usually looks like a chain link ðŸ”—)
3. Paste the URL (web address)
4. Click "Add" or "Apply"

**Example:**
"Visit our [Facebook page](https://www.facebook.com/chriscakesofmi) for daily updates!"

### SEO Settings

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) helps people find your page on Google.

**Meta Title**
- The title that appears in search results
- Usually 50-60 characters
- Example: "About ChrisCakes | Premier Breakfast Caterer in Michigan"

**Meta Description**
- The description in search results
- Usually 150-160 characters
- Example: "ChrisCakes has been serving pancakes by the millions since 1969! Premier breakfast and event catering for groups from 50 to 50,000 in Michigan."

**Tips:**
- Include important keywords naturally
- Make it compelling to encourage clicks
- Be accurate about what's on the page

### Step 3: Publish Your Changes

1. Review your edits
2. Click **"Publish"** at the bottom
3. Wait 60 seconds for the website to update
4. Visit the page on your website to verify changes

## Common Page Updates

### Updating Your About Page

Your About page tells your story. Consider including:
- When you started your business (since 1969!)
- Your background and experience
- What makes you special/different (pancakes by the millions!)
- Your values or mission
- Personal touches (family business, passion for serving people, etc.)

**Example structure:**
```
Welcome to ChrisCakes (Heading 2)
[Introduction paragraph about your catering business]

Our Story (Heading 2)
[How and when you started - serving since 1969!]

What Makes Us Special (Heading 2)
[What sets you apart - all-you-can-eat pancakes, large event expertise, etc.]

- All-you-can-eat pancake breakfasts
- Events from 50 to 50,000 people
- Professional catering equipment
- Experienced staff
[Bulleted list of key points]
```

### Updating Your Services Page

List what you offer:
- Types of catering packages (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
- Menu options and customization
- Event setup and service
- Group size capabilities (50 to 50,000!)
- Pricing information (if you share it)

**Tips:**
- Be specific about what you offer
- Mention any minimum group sizes or requirements
- Include a call-to-action: "Contact us for a free quote!"

### Updating FAQ Page

Common questions might include:
- How far in advance should I book?
- What size groups can you serve?
- Do you provide serving staff?
- What are your prices?
- Can you accommodate dietary restrictions?

**Format:**
Use headings for questions, regular text for answers:

```
How far in advance should I book? (Heading 3)
We recommend booking at least 2-4 weeks in advance for most events. For large events (500+ people) or peak seasons, please book 6-8 weeks ahead. However, we'll do our best to accommodate last-minute requests when possible!

What size groups can you serve? (Heading 3)
We can cater events from 50 to 50,000 people! Whether you're planning a small school breakfast or a massive community event, we have the equipment and experience to handle it.
```

## Creating a New Page

1. Click **"+ Create"**
2. Select **"Page"**
3. Fill in:
   - **Title**: The page name (appears at top of page and in browser tab)
   - **Slug**: URL for the page (auto-generated from title)
   - **Content**: The page content (use the rich text editor)
   - **SEO**: Meta title and description for search engines
4. Click **"Publish"**

**Note**: New pages won't automatically appear in your navigation menu. You may need to contact your web developer to add them to the menu.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I preview my changes before publishing?**
A: The Studio may have a preview option. Look for a "Preview" button. Otherwise, you can save as a draft, publish, wait 60 seconds, check the live site, then edit again if needed.

**Q: I want to add a video. How do I do that?**
A: You typically can't upload videos directly. Instead:
1. Upload your video to YouTube or Vimeo
2. Get the share/embed link
3. Add that link to your page text, or contact your developer about embedding it

**Q: How do I change the order of sections on a page?**
A: You'll need to cut and paste text to reorganize. Select the section you want to move, cut it (Ctrl+X / Cmd+X), click where you want it to go, and paste (Ctrl+V / Cmd+V).

**Q: Can I use colors or different fonts?**
A: This depends on your website's setup. Most content editors use your site's predefined styles to keep everything consistent. If you need special formatting, contact your web developer.

**Q: My page looks different than I expected.**
A: The editor shows basic formatting. The actual website applies additional styling (colors, fonts, spacing) automatically. Your content will look more polished on the live site!

**Q: How often should I update my pages?**
A:
- Update immediately if information changes (prices, services, contact info)
- Refresh content every 6-12 months to keep it current
- Add seasonal updates if relevant to your business

**Q: What if I accidentally delete important content?**

## Writing Tips

### Keep It Clear and Concise
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Break up long sections with headings
- Use bullet points for lists
- Avoid jargon or overly technical language

### Focus on Your Customer
- Answer "What's in it for me?" from the customer's perspective
- Use "you" and "your" more than "we" and "our"
- Include specific benefits, not just features

**Feature**: "We use premium ingredients"
**Benefit**: "You'll taste the difference in every bite with our premium ingredients"

### Include Calls-to-Action
Tell people what to do next:
- "Contact us for a free consultation"
- "Call today to schedule your cake tasting"
- "Visit our menu to see all our offerings"

### Proofread Before Publishing
- Check for spelling and grammar errors
- Read it out loudâ€”does it sound natural?
- Have someone else review it if possible
- Make sure all links work
